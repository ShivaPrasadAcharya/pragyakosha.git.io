B2

Basic Structure Doctrine

Keshavananda Bharati Case:

• Longest marathon 68 days hearing.

• Largest bench in the history of Supreme Court of India (13 Justices) led by CJ Sikri and the judgement was delivered on the last working day of CJ Sikri. Upon his retirement the tradition of appointing the senior most judge as Chief Justice was abandoned and Justice Ray, who was fourth in the line of seniority, superseded other 3 justices and all three of them later resigned.

• This case was heard for 6 months from October 31, 1972- March 23, 1973. ( Monday, Wednesday and Thursday)

• Nani Palkhiwala was the leading lawyer teamed with CK Daphtary, MC Chagla and was assisted by Soli Soarabjee, Anil Divan DM Popat, and Fali Nariman and others. For Keshavananda Bharati.

• Funding of the litigation was done by Coal, Mining and Sugar Company.

• Lawyers were kept at Oberoi Hotel during hearing days.

• Counter team was led by HM Seervai ( State of Kerala) and Niren De ( AG UOI).

• Palkhiwala and his. team argued for 31 days. Palkhiwala rejoined pleaded for 2 days.

• HM Seervai argued for 21 days Niren Dde (AG of India) and team argued for 12 days.

• Longest judgement of 703 pages.

• Golakhnath was overruled by 9 of the 13 judges ( there was division of justices in various points to by 7:6).

• Ratio Power of the Parliament to amend the Constitution would not include the power to alter its basic structure or essential features- Basic Structure Doctrine.

• By this case Supreme Court strengthen the power of judicial review and ipso facto, placed a limitation on the parliament's power to amend the constitution. This is the case from which the reputation and recognition of the constitutional lawyers was heighten.

• That time Indira Gandhi was the Prime Minister of India with stunning victory, gathering almost 350 seats out of the total strength of 540 and has pressing interest of Government in this case. Justice Jagmohan (One among 13) said, “ I got the impression from the first day of hearing that minds of justices were closed and views were determined."
